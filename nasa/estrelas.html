Red giants are stars in the late stages of their evolution, having exhausted the hydrogen in their cores. They are characterized by their enormous size, often expanding to hundreds of times the Sun's diameter. Despite their intense brightness, red giants have relatively low surface temperatures, typically between 2,500 and 5,000 K, which gives them their characteristic red or orange color. Their core becomes extremely dense and hot, starting to fuse heavier elements like helium, while the outer layers expand.
